🍔 Grill & Measure – The Fraction Feast! ✨

A Sizzling STEM Adventure in Measurements and Fractions!

Welcome, Young Chefs!

Oh what a delightful idea, dearie! Cooking on the grill and learning measurements and fractions—why, that’s a recipe for success if I’ve ever seen one! Let's whip up a lovely STEM class that blends math, science, and deliciousness—all under the aroma of sizzling burgers!

Grade Level: 4th–6th

Duration: ~60–75 minutes

Objective: Students will learn basic measurement concepts and fractions through hands-on cooking and portioning activities using hamburger meat on a propane grill.

🧪 STEM Focus Areas:

  • Science: States of matter, heat transfer, temperature control
  • Technology/Engineering: Grill operation and safety, propane use
  • Math: Measurements (volume, weight), fractions (halves, thirds, quarters), conversions

1. Warm-Up (10 minutes) — Fraction Foundations

Let's review some key terms: whole, half, third, quarter, ounce (oz), pound (lb).

Imagine a yummy chocolate bar! If you break it into 2 equal pieces, each piece is a half (1/2). If you break it into 4 equal pieces, each piece is a quarter (1/4).

Quick Question: If we had 1 lb of meat and split it into 4 equal burgers, how much would each burger weigh in pounds (as a fraction)?

Hint: 1 pound = 16 ounces. So each burger would also be 4 oz!

2. Science of Cooking (10 minutes)

When we cook, science is all around us!

  • Heat Transfer: Heat moves from the hot grill to the cold burger!
    • Conduction: Heat moving directly through the grill grates to the burger.
    • Convection: Hot air circulating around the burger.
  • What happens to meat?
    • Proteins change shape and firm up (that's why it goes from soft to firm).
    • Fats melt and make it juicy (this is called rendering).
    • Water evaporates, making steam.

4. Grill Time! (15 minutes, supervised by adult)

If we were really grilling, safety would be super important!

  • Always have an adult supervise.
  • Be careful of the heat and open flames.
  • Know how to use the propane tank safely.
  • Use a food thermometer to check if the burgers are cooked. For ground beef, it needs to reach an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C) to be safe to eat.

In our virtual grill, imagine the patties sizzling away! Our 3D scene shows a simple grill.

5. Math Wrap-Up (10 minutes) – The Burger Breakdown

Fractions are everywhere in cooking!

  • Toppings:
    • You might cut a slice of cheese in half (1/2).
    • You might slice a tomato into eighths (1/8).
    • You might use 1/4 cup of onions.
  • Portion Sizes: We've already seen how burgers are portioned using fractions of a pound (1/4 lb, 1/3 lb).

Think about your favorite recipe. Where do you see fractions or measurements?
